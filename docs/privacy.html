Privacy Policy

Last updated: May 6, 2026

This Privacy Policy describes how Molar ("we," "us," or "our") collects, uses, and protects information in connection with our dental practice management platform.

Information We Collect

We collect information provided directly by dental practices and their patients, including names, contact information, appointment details, and clinical records necessary to operate the platform.

SMS Communications

Molar sends appointment reminders and practice communications via SMS on behalf of dental practices using our platform. The following terms govern SMS consent and messaging.

How consent is obtained: Verbal consent is collected by front-desk staff during scheduling calls. Staff read the following script to patients:

"We'd like to send you automated text reminders about your upcoming appointments and care instructions. These messages may include appointment confirmations, reminders, and follow-up information. Would you like to receive text messages from us at the number you've provided? You can reply STOP at any time to stop receiving messages. Message and data rates may apply."

Upon patient agreement, consent is recorded in the patient's chart with the date, time, and name of the staff member who collected it. A confirmation text is then sent immediately to the patient's mobile number.

Types of messages sent:

  • Appointment confirmations and reminders
  • Two-way confirmation requests (e.g., reply CONFIRM or CANCEL)
  • Post-appointment care instructions
  • Recall and recare reminders

Message frequency: Typically 1–3 messages per scheduled appointment.

Message and data rates: Standard message and data rates from your carrier may apply.

Opt-out: Reply STOP to any message to immediately unsubscribe. You will receive one confirmation message and no further messages will be sent. To re-subscribe, contact your dental practice directly.

Help: Reply HELP to any message for assistance, or contact us at g.s.garber@outlook.com.

Data Security

We implement industry-standard security measures to protect patient and practice data. Data is encrypted in transit and at rest. We do not sell patient data to third parties.

HIPAA

Molar operates as a Business Associate under HIPAA. All protected health information (PHI) is handled in accordance with applicable federal and state privacy regulations.

Contact

For questions about this policy or our data practices, contact us at g.s.garber@outlook.com.
