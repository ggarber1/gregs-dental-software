# tab32 (tab32, Inc.)

## Overview

**Company:** tab32, Inc.
**Market position:** ~8–11% cloud market share; growing fast among tech-forward and specialty practices
**Architecture:** 100% cloud-native (Google Cloud Platform)
**Scale (2024):** 17M+ appointments, 13M+ patients, 100M+ dental X-rays, $375M annual revenue through platform, 1M+ monthly patient engagements
**Capterra rating:** ~4.1–4.6/5 (limited reviews: ~41–80)
**Notable:** Inc. 5000 Fastest Growing Companies — 2 consecutive years (2024)

## Pricing

tab32 does **not publish standard pricing**. All pricing via sales demo/quote.

### Estimated Monthly Costs (third-party sources)
| Users | Est. Monthly |
|-------|-------------|
| 1 user (solo) | ~$199/month |
| ~10 users | ~$999/month |
| ~100 users | ~$2,499/month |
| 1,000+ users (enterprise) | Custom pricing |

### Critical Post-2024 Change: Per-Use AI Fees
After rebuilding the platform around AI, tab32 introduced **per-use fees layered on top of subscriptions** for:
- Per-insurance-eligibility-check fees
- Per-clinical-note AI fees
- Other AI-powered touchpoints billed individually

This shift has generated significant user backlash because **costs become opaque and unpredictable**. Practices cannot budget consistently.

### Implementation
- Estimated $500–$2,000 depending on size and migration complexity
- Larger multi-location migrations will exceed this significantly

### Training
- In theory included; in practice: video libraries + brief onboarding call
- One-on-one live support available only during Eastern Time Zone business hours
- No free trial offered

## Features

### Scheduling
- Drag-and-drop calendar with real-time availability
- Automated appointment reminders (text, email)
- Multi-location and cross-location scheduling
- Online patient self-scheduling
- Recall/reactivation management

### Clinical / EHR
- Dental and periodontal charting (designed by dentists)
- Treatment planning with explosion codes and prompts
- Progress notes, auto-notes, exam forms
- eRx (electronic prescriptions)
- Customizable form templates and consent forms
- Alerts and patient health history
- Multi-specialty support (GP, pediatric, orthodontics)

### Imaging / Radiology
- Hardware-agnostic: works with most radiographic sensor brands
- Cloud-based image storage (100M+ X-rays on platform)
- **Pearl Second Opinion AI integration** (announced June 2024): real-time detection of caries, bone loss, root abscesses, and other pathologies
- Panoramic and bitewing support

### Billing / Revenue Cycle
- Insurance eligibility verification (real-time; now fee-per-check under new AI model)
- Claims submission with e-attachments
- ERA/EOB processing
- In-house payment plan management
- Accounts receivable tracking
- Revenue discovery tools
- KPI and billing dashboards

### Patient Engagement
- Two-way SMS texting
- Automated appointment reminders and recall
- Pre-visit digital intake forms
- Reputation management and review generation
- Marketing email campaigns
- Patient portal

### Reporting / Analytics — A Key Differentiator
- Standard practice KPI dashboards
- **Open Data Warehouse:** Purpose-built for DSOs — aggregates PMS, HR, vendor, and lab data
- **Business intelligence connectors:** Tableau, Microsoft Power BI, Google Data Studio, Looker
- OLAP + OLTP analytics (online analytical + transactional processing)
- Machine learning model support for multi-location data sets

### API / Platform
- **Comprehensive public API** (SOC2 and HIPAA compliant)
- Enables third-party vendors to build on top of tab32
- Supports patient communication, charting, scheduling, imaging, and analytics endpoints
- Positioned as a platform layer, not just an application

## Target Market

tab32 explicitly targets three segments:
1. **Solo/Independent practices** — cloud-native alternative to server-based incumbents
2. **Group practices (3–50 locations)** — centralized records, cross-location scheduling, group reporting
3. **DSOs and enterprise** — open data warehouse, BI integrations, enterprise architecture on Google Cloud

Featured DSO customer: **Lightwave Dental** (multi-location DSO) deployed tab32 at scale.

Also targets: **mobile dental practices** and **multi-specialty groups** (GP, pediatric, orthodontics).

## User Reviews

### Praise
- **Charting designed intuitively** — doctors praise the clinician-centric design
- Cloud access from anywhere is highly valued by multi-location practices
- In-program insurance eligibility verification praised
- **Open data warehouse and BI tools** get strong marks from DSO administrators
- Original UI was considered clean and accessible
- Pearl AI integration adds real clinical value

### Complaints
- **Post-AI-platform transition: no emergency live support** — users cannot get help when X-ray fails with a patient in the chair
- **Per-use AI fees are unpredictable** — cost cannot be budgeted consistently
- No mobile app
- Perio charting requires mouse instead of keyboard — slows hygienists
- System performance degrades with internet slowdowns
- Appointment scheduling window frequently freezes
- X-ray quality described as poor and disorganized in some configurations
- **Training inadequate** — mostly video-based; one-on-one only during ET business hours
- No free trial — high purchase friction

---

## Customer Support

- **Quality trend:** Mixed to declining post-AI transition
- **Pre-transition:** Widely praised
- **Post-transition:** Shifted toward AI-automated responses and self-service; degraded experience
- **Response times:** 24–48 hours for simple questions; weeks for ongoing issues
- **During clinical emergencies:** Users explicitly report being unable to get live support when X-ray systems fail mid-appointment — sharpest complaint
- **Channels:** Phone, email/ticket system, video library
- **Hours:** No 24/7 live support; live support only during ET business hours
- West Coast practices are disadvantaged by ET-only hours

## Integration Ecosystem

## Key Weaknesses Summary

1. **No mobile app** — complete gap in a mobile-first world
2. **Per-use AI fees create unpredictable billing** — practices cannot budget
3. **Support has degraded post-AI transition** — no emergency live support during patient care
4. **Perio charting is keyboard-unfriendly** — slows hygienists materially
5. **Internet dependency** — cloud-only; outages leave practice dead in the water
6. **Steep learning curve** with inadequate training resources
7. **Opaque pricing** — no public rates; requires sales engagement
8. **No free trial** — high purchase friction
9. **East Coast support hours** — disadvantages West Coast practices
10. **Limited named integration marketplace** — less turnkey than Dentrix or Open Dental ecosystems
