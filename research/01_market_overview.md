## Key Market Trends (2024–2026)

1. **Server → Cloud migration** accelerating. Eaglesoft (server-only) is losing ground; Dentrix Ascend, Curve, CareStack, Denticon are winning conversions.
2. **DSO consolidation** is the dominant growth driver. DSOs represent 25%+ of US practices and growing. Half of all dental tech VC in 2025 went to DSO-enabling platforms.
3. **AI integration** moving from bolt-on to native. FDA-cleared AI (Pearl, VideaHealth, Overjet) being embedded directly into PMS workflows for radiograph analysis, insurance verification, scheduling, and clinical notes.
4. **All-in-one vs. best-of-breed tension**. Legacy vendors charge per module; new entrants (Archy, CareStack) bundle everything. The market is rewarding consolidation.
5. **Pricing model disruption**. Flat-rate all-inclusive pricing (Archy at $299/month) is gaining traction vs. opaque per-module legacy pricing. Archy reports average savings of $8,000/year vs. incumbents.
6. **Teledentistry mainstreaming**. Visits grew from <50,000 (2019) to 4.1 million (2024); payers reimburse at 85% of in-office rate. Yet almost no PMS has native teledentistry.

## Practice Landscape

- ~200,000 dental practices in the US
- Solo practices: ~60% of market
- Group practices (2–10 locations): ~25%
- DSOs (10+ locations): ~15% and growing rapidly
- Average dental practice uses 8–12 separate software tools (PMS, imaging, patient comms, analytics, payments, etc.)

## The Core Problem for Incumbents

Legacy vendors (Dentrix, Eaglesoft) were built in the 1990s–2000s on client-server Windows architecture. They have enormous installed bases and deep distributor networks — but:

- Their UI/UX is universally described as outdated
- They charge per-module, making true cost 2–3x the listed price
- They require expensive on-premise IT infrastructure
- They have no native mobile apps
- They are resistant to migrating customers to cloud (it cannibalizes hardware/IT revenue)

This creates a significant opening for a well-designed cloud-native entrant.
